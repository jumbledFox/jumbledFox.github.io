LED Flashing light mask

This is something I made for my mum a little while ago. It's an eye mask that flashes red LEDs at a user-controlled speed and brightness. Now, I'm no medical expert, so I have no clue why she wanted it, but according to her it's something that helps relieve stress, so I went and made it.

Parts

I began by ordering a bunch of parts online from Bitsboxuk my beloved, and a little while later they all showed up.

These parts included a DC converting plug for power, better potentiometers, a switch, and the ATtiny 13 that would run the code.

Starting off - The case

I started off by making the case. I ordered a nice little box and began work drilling holes.

Ready to drill! :3

And, after a long night of planning and drilling..

Ok.. now the box is finished... what to do with this little guy?

Circuitry

I started off by making the circuit on a breadboard with my arduino. I wrote some code to read the value of the speed potentiometer and BOOM, led flashing.

But.. how am I gonna get this onto my attiny?

Programming the ATtiny

So, I laid out my arduino ready to program the ATtiny..

..but, nothing? It's not uploading! Why not??!?!

And.. after many hours... I realised I had to burn the bootloader program to the arduino....

And, now I can program the ATtiny..

Yippee!

Ok, I have the ATtiny programmed, the circuit crudely drawn out, now to start combining the case and the circuit.

Circuitry II

I soldered many wires to the different knobs and levers

And then I soldered them to the breadboard!

I cut the mask part off of a red light mask my mum already owned that didn't flash, and attached it to the above mess.

Before finishing it off I went out for a nice bike ride

And then, I finally closed it up for good! Here's a video of it in action, on my mothers bedside table.
